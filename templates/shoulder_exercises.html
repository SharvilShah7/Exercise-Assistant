Shoulder Exercises

Double Dumbbell Shoulder Press

The double dumbbell shoulder press is a compound resistance exercise that targets the deltoid muscles of the shoulders, as well as engaging the triceps and upper chest. It involves holding a dumbbell in each hand at shoulder height and pushing them upwards until the arms are fully extended overhead, then lowering the weights back to the starting position. This exercise helps improve shoulder strength, stability, and muscle definition, contributing to overall upper body.

Shoulder Lateral Raise

The shoulder lateral raise is an isolation exercise primarily targeting the deltoid muscles of the shoulders. It involves holding dumbbells at the sides with palms facing the body and lifting the arms outward to the sides until they are parallel to the ground, then lowering them back down. This exercise helps strengthen and define the shoulders, particularly the lateral deltoid head, improving shoulder stability and enhancing upper body aesthetics.

Shoulder Upright Rows

The shoulder upright row is a compound exercise primarily targeting the deltoid muscles of the shoulders, along with the trapezius and upper back muscles. It involves holding a barbell or dumbbells with an overhand grip, hands positioned slightly closer than shoulder-width apart, and lifting the weight upwards towards the chin while keeping the elbows higher than the forearms. This exercise helps to improve shoulder and upper back strength.

Alternate Front Raises

The alternate front raise is an isolation exercise that primarily targets the anterior deltoid muscles of the shoulders. It involves holding dumbbells in each hand, palms facing towards the body, and lifting one arm at a time straight forward until it reaches shoulder height, while keeping the other arm stationary. This exercise helps to strengthen and define the front portion of the shoulders, improving shoulder stability and enhancing overall upper body aesthetics.
