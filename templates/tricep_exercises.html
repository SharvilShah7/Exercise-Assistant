Tricep Exercises

Triceps (Cable Push Down)

The triceps cable push-down is an isolation exercise that primarily targets the triceps muscles. It involves using a cable machine with a straight or angled bar attachment, gripping the bar with an overhand grip, and pushing it downward until the arms are fully extended, focusing on contracting the triceps. This exercise helps to strengthen and define the triceps, promoting arm stability and improving overall upper body strength.

Standing Overhead Dumbbell Extension

The Standing Overhead Dumbbell Extension is an isolation exercise that targets the triceps muscles. It involves standing with a dumbbell held overhead, elbow bent and close to the head, and then straightening the arm to lift the weight directly upward. This exercise effectively works the triceps, aiding in muscle strength and definition, particularly in the long head of the triceps.

Arm Kickback

The Arm Kickback exercise is an isolation movement primarily targeting the triceps muscles. It involves bending at the waist, holding a dumbbell in one hand, and extending the arm straight back behind the body while keeping the elbow stationary. This action contracts the triceps, promoting strength and definition in the back of the upper arm. It's a unilateral exercise, meaning it works one arm at a time.

Bench Dips

The bench dip is a bodyweight exercise that primarily targets the triceps, chest, and shoulders. It involves sitting on the edge of a bench with hands placed on the bench behind the body, fingers facing forward, and legs extended out in front. By lowering the body towards the ground and then pushing back up, the triceps are engaged to lift the body weight. Bench dips are an effective way to strengthen and tone the triceps muscles without the need for additional equipment, contributing to overall upper body strength and definition.
